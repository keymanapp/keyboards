To type a character whose key has been repurposed (such as the semicolon), use hold AltGr (Right alt/opt) and press that key (e.g. AltGr + ; for a semicolon, AltGr + W for "W", etc). This layout is also compatible with Toaq Beta and Gamma, although some diacritics may be rather inconvenient to type.

On Windows, to associate the keyboard with a different language, which may be useful if it automatically associated itself with Dutch for example, but you want it to show up under English, open the Keyman Configuration and under "Toaqzu (Hóegua)", select "Add/Remove language..." and choose the language you want. Don't forget to remove the original language as well.

For the purposes of typing Robin's Koamtoaq, it is also possible to type underdots on letters with macrons and hooks above.

Mobile

The mobile layout is similar to QWERTY mobile keyboards with a few differences.
Cheatsheet
Without underdot With underdot
Falling tone (◌) vowel Input vowel first, then tap the ◌̣ key (to the right of L)
Rising tone (◌́) Swipe up on vowel
Glottal tone (◌̈) Swipe sideways on vowel(both work in case one is more convenient)
Hiatus tone (◌̂) Swipe down on vowel
W, i Long press on Ꝡ, ı

One can also access the grave diacritic for Prefix Reform by long-pressing on the underdot key.
