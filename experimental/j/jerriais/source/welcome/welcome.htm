Start Using Jèrriais

A keyboard for typing in Jèrriais, a dialect of Norman and the native language of Jersey in the Channel Islands.

If you're on using a smartphone, remember to install the Jèrriais wordlist package as well as this keyboard package. You need both packages installed to get autocomplete for Jèrriais!

Smartphone Version

The smartphone keyboard layout is similar to English. You'll notice that the vowels and some other letters have dots in the top right corner; this means you can hold down this key to find other options. For example, holding down a vowel gives accent options, and holding down a punctuation key gives further punctuation. Keep holding your finger down, slide to the desired option and release.

You can also use flicking gestures for commonly used accents.

  • Flicking up on a vowel gives a circumflex
  • Flicking up and to the right on an appropriate vowel gives an acute accent
  • Flicking down and to the right on an appropriate vowel gives a grave accent
  • Flicking to the left gives a diaresis
  • Flicking down on the c key gives a cedilla
  • Flicking left on the full stop gives a comma
  • Flicking up on the apostrophe gives a double quote

Other punctuation can be found by pressing the 123 key.

Desktop Version

On a desktop computer you use your keyboard normally, until you want to use an accent:

  • vowel followed by two single quotes ('') creates an acute accent: e'' = é
  • vowel followed by two backticks (``) creates a grave accent: a`` = à
  • vowel (or "y") followed by two carets (^^) creates a circumflex: o^^ = ô
  • vowel followed by two double quotes ("") creates a diaresis: i"" = ï
  • a "c" followed by two commas (,,) creates a cedilla: c,, = ç
