Ezidi

The Ezidi script has a long history and it is used to write Ezidi language (also called Ezdiki) in Caucasian its official language in the Republic of Armenia and Russia where it has its curriculum, According to some data, this alphabet dates back to the 13–14th centuries and was used to write two manuscripts, Maṣḥaf Raš and Ktébī Jalwe̱h, first published by Anastase Marie (1911) Traces of this language are still present in many manuscripts and archaeological evidence, one of the manuscripts of a Ezidi holy book is located in the Austrian museum and it was written in the Ezidi language, These letters were proposed to Unicode 13 and released on March 10-2020, and after the release of the letters, Ezidi young sought to create a unified keyboard to write with it on social media, as they opened public pages and groups and for chatting on social media sites such as Facebook, TikTok, X(Twitter), etc.
