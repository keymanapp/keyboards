Anglish Dvorak keyboard
==============

Description
-----------
Dvorak Layout Keyboard for Ænglish (Old English). Unlike, the other Old English Keyboards on Keyman, this one only has the Old English Original Alphabets, and not Modern English Ones. Secondly, it is has a dedicated Phone Interface, not just Auto-Generated from Desktop Layout. Featuring letters, like, æ (ash), þ (thorn), ƿ (wynn), ȝ (yogh), and ð (eth), that where once in Proper Ænglish. With the exception of æ, all are taken from Old Runic Alphabet, that Ænglish was written before the Latin Alphabet. Due to lack of Runic Characters in the Printing Press, that was brought from Europe, 'ƿ' was replaced with 'w', 'ȝ' was replaced with 'g' and, 'þ' and 'ð' were replaced with 'th'. Moreover, this was before 'I' was divided into 'I' and 'J', and 'V' was divided into 'U', 'V', and 'W', so 'J', 'K', 'Q', 'V', 'W', and 'Z' didn't exist in Ænglish, and hence, doesn't exist in this aswell. Both 'F' and 'S' had two sounds Voiced and Unvoiced.
