Start Using Bunong Latin (SIL)

Bunong Latin (SIL) keyboard is designed for typing Bunong language using Latin characters. This keyboard produces NFD characters.

Keyboard Layout

This keyboard makes use of multiple keystrokes to produce one character. For example:

  • Typing "7" after a vowel will produce a horn character. a7 will produce ơ (available on oOuU).
  • Typing "9" after a b or d will produce a barred character. b9 will produce ƀ (available on bBdD).
  • ;b will produce ƀ and ;d will produce đ (available on bBdD).
  • Typing "6" after a vowel will produce a circumflex accent. a6 will produce â (available on aAeEoO). Typing 66 will produce a 6.
  • Typing "8" after a vowel will produce a breve accent. a8 will produce ă (available on aAeEiIoOuUơƠưƯ). Typing 8 will produce a 8.
  • Typing "<" after a vowel will produce an acute accent. a< will produce á. (available on aAeEiIoOuU).
  • Typing "`" after a vowel will produce an grave accent. a` will produce à. (available on aAeEiIoOuU).
  • Typing "~" after a vowel will produce an hook above accent. a~ will produce ả. (available on aAeEiIoOuU).
  • Typing ">" after a vowel will produce a dot below accent. a> will produce ạ. (available on aAeEiIoOuU).
  • Typing the same character a second time will produce a basic character. For example, typing "a<<"" will produce "a'" and "a>>" will produce "a/". Similarly, "a``" and "a~~" will produce "a`" and "a~" respectively.

In addition, the following keystrokes can be used to produce characters:

  • ;' = “
  • ;" = ”
  • ;;" = «
  • ;;" = »
  • RALT SPACE = hair space
  • RALT SHIFT SPACE = thin space
  • LCTRL SPACE = four per em space
  • LCTRL SHIFT SPACE = en space
